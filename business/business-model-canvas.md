# Business Model Canvas for Coordination-Watchdog

## Customer Segments
- AI development teams in tech companies (startups to enterprises).
- Research institutions focusing on AI and multi-agent systems.
- DevOps teams responsible for maintaining AI production environments.
- Industries relying on AI for critical operations (e.g., finance, healthcare).

## Value Propositions
- Real-time monitoring of multi-agent AI systems to prevent coordination failures.
- Enhanced debugging capabilities to quickly identify and resolve silent loops.
- Increased reliability of AI systems, reducing downtime and operational risks.
- User-friendly interface for visualizing agent interactions and performance metrics.

## Channels
- Direct sales through the company website and dedicated sales team.
- Partnerships with AI development platforms and cloud service providers.
- Online marketing through tech blogs, webinars, and industry conferences.
- GitHub repository for open-source contributions and community engagement.

## Customer Relationships
- Dedicated customer support for troubleshooting and onboarding.
- Community forums for user collaboration and feedback.
- Regular updates and newsletters to keep customers informed about new features.
- Training sessions and workshops for teams to maximize product usage.

## Revenue Streams
- Subscription model: Monthly/annual plans starting at $99/month (approx. 3,500 THB) for small teams, scaling up to $999/month (approx. 35,000 THB) for enterprise solutions.
- One-time licensing fees for on-premise installations.
- Consulting services for custom integrations and optimizations.
- Revenue from partnerships and co-development projects with other AI tools.

## Key Resources
- Development team with expertise in AI and software engineering.
- Robust monitoring and debugging technology stack.
- Customer support and training personnel.
- Marketing and sales team for outreach and engagement.

## Key Activities
- Continuous development and improvement of the coordination-watchdog tool.
- Conducting market research to identify emerging needs and trends.
- Building partnerships with AI platforms and communities.
- Providing customer support and training to ensure user satisfaction.

## Key Partners
- AI technology providers (e.g., cloud services, development platforms).
- Research institutions for collaborative development and testing.
- Industry associations for networking and visibility.
- Open-source communities for contributions and feedback.

## Cost Structure
- Salaries for development, support, and marketing teams.
- Infrastructure costs for hosting and maintaining the software.
- Marketing and sales expenses for outreach and customer acquisition.
- Research and development costs for ongoing product enhancements.
